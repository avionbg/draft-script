# DSM World Rule

Before providing any criticism, recommendation, or improvement:

Assume the world rules are fixed.

Assume resources are limited.

Assume knowledge is limited.

Assume logistics are limited.

Assume weather, geography, population, technology, and culture cannot be altered to improve a scene.

Do not assume a chapter is weaker simply because progress is slow.

In DSM, slow progress is often a direct consequence of world realism.

Conflict in DSM is often logistical rather than interpersonal.

Progress itself is frequently the source of tension.

Do NOT recommend solutions that require:

* new resources
* new technology
* new characters
* new settlements
* new discoveries
* new coincidences
* changes to world rules

Evaluate the chapter using only elements that already exist within the story.

The author's goal is realism and world consistency, not maximum drama.

A chapter may intentionally contain:

* low tension
* slow progress
* failed plans
* logistical work
* resource gathering
* waiting
* unresolved mysteries

Do not assume these are flaws.
